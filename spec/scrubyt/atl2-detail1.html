Job Title: Asset Protection & Investigation Manager
Closing Date/Time: Fri. 06/07/13 11:59 PM Eastern Time
Salary: $54,700.00 - $91,100.00 Annually
Job Type: Full-time
Location: Atlanta, GA, Georgia
 
Print Job Information | Apply  

 
Description  Benefits  Supplemental Questions
 
The purpose of this job is to provide investigative legal services for the City of Atlanta. Responsibilities include managing a team of trained staff; conducting investigations; preparing investigation reports for use in court and administrative hearings; testifying in court and in administrative hearings when necessary; preparing investigative summaries and various other reports; etc.

 Examples Of Essential Duties:
The following duties are normal for this job. These are not to be construed as exclusive or all-inclusive. Other duties may be required and assigned. 

Work Delegation:

Supervises, directs and evaluates assigned staff, handling employee concerns and problems, directing work, counseling, disciplining and completing employee performance appraisals. Plans each investigation so as to identify crucial evidence for preparation of conclusions and recommendations. Delegates work to subordinates as necessary to ensure that the integrity of evidence collected is maintained at all times. 

Administrative Duties:

Investigates the affairs of the department, bureau, or division as it relates to an action in question that may go against a DWM or City policy, code, or law at the direction of the DWM commissioner. Uses resource materials such as the following to assist in investigations: Official Code of GA. Ann., City Charter, City Ordinance, and Departmental Policies and Procedures. Investigates the affairs of any person who has/had dealings with the City or its agencies at the direction of the Commissioner. Investigates the conduct of any City employee, person, firm, or corporation dealing with the City at the direction of the DWM Commissioner. Assists attorneys in writing questions for depositions, interviews, etc at the direction of the DWM Commissioner. Testifies as expert witness in court and administrative hearings. 

Communication:

Maintains on-going relationships with area law enforcement agencies. Prepares investigations for use before courts. 

Quality Assurance:

Creates accurate and well documented investigative reports. Maintains high quality communication and interacts with all City personnel, supervisors, City Council members, contractors, and vendors. Exercises immediate remedial action to correct any possible or real problem. 

Record Keeping and Documentation:

Prepares investigative summaries and sends to requestor; prepares status reports and makes presentations thereof; prepares correspondence and briefs; and prepares flow charts to be used in discussions/presentations. Reviews and investigates: lost/stolen city property reports, employee grievances, contractor's misconduct, and contractor's breach of contract. Prepares various reports during and at the conclusion of an investigation.

Marginal Job Functions

Performs other related duties as required.invalidinvalidinvalidinvalid
 Typical Qualifications:
Bachelor’s Degree in Criminal Justice, Criminology, or closely related field. POST certification not required but highly recommended and preferred; five years of progressively responsible supervisory investigative job experience; or any equivalent combination of education, training, and experience which provides the requisite knowledge, skills, and abilities for this job. Experience working in and with law enforcement agencies such as Police, Sheriffs and DA’s office that deal with multiple types of investigations. Field Training Officer experience preferred.
 Supplemental Information:
The City of Atlanta is an Equal Opportunity Employer.
